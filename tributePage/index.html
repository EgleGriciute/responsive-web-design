Freddie Mercury

was a British singer, songwriter, record producer, and lead vocalist of the rock band Queen

Here's a time line of Freddie Mercury's life:

  • 1946 - Freddie Mercury was born in Stone Town, Zanzibar (Tanzania)
  • 1953 - Freddie began taking piano lessons
  • 1958 - Freddie formed ‘The Hectics’ a school band that played covers of popular songs. Around this time he began to use the christian name Freddie
  • 1970 - The group Queen made their first performance
  • 1973 - Queen released their first album entitled ‘Queen’. It reached number 83 in the US and number 47 in the UK
  • 1974 - Queen released their second album Queen II. It reached number 5 in the UK charts and number 49 in the US
  • 1975 - Queen released the single ‘Bohemian Rhapsody’. It stayed at number 1 in the UK charts for 9 consecutive weeks and sold over a million copies. It reached number 9 in the US charts. It was also the first single to be released with a music video
  • 1977 - The single ‘We Are The Champions’ with ‘We Will Rock You’ as the B side, was released. It reached number 2 in the UK and number 4 in the US. Both tracks were taken from the album ‘News Of The World’
  • 1980 - The song ‘Another One Bites The Dust’ from the album ‘The Game’ was released as a single. It reached number 7 in the UK and number 1 in the US
  • 1981 - The group began a tour of South America, the first rock group to tour the continent
  • 1985 - Queen performed some of their greatest hits at the Live Aid concert held at Wembley Stadium, London. Their performance has been voted one of the greatest live rock music performances of all time
  • 1987 - Freddie Mercury was diagnosed with AIDS. The diagnosis was kept from the public
  • 1990 - Freddie Mercury denied rumours that he was very ill
  • 1991 - Freddie Mercury died of bronchial pneumonia which he had contracted as a complication of AIDS
